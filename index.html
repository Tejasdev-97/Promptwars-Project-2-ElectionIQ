Special Guide: Rural & First-Time Voters

Voting in village Panchayat or Municipal elections? Voting for the very first time? Here is what you need to know:

1. Gram Panchayat (Rural Elections)

Unlike big elections (EVMs), village Panchayat elections often still use Paper Ballots. You will get a ballot paper and a rubber stamp. Stamp clearly over your candidate's symbol, fold the paper securely, and drop it in the ballot box.

2. First-Time Voters (18+)

You can apply for your Voter ID by filling Form 6 via the Voter Helpline App. Even if your physical card hasn't arrived by election day, if your name is on the list, you can vote using your Aadhaar card or PAN card.

3. Assistance at the Booth

If you cannot read the ballot or are physically challenged, don't worry. The Presiding Officer is trained to assist you. You are also allowed to bring a companion (18+) to help you press the button or stamp the paper.

Types of Elections in India

India's democratic framework consists of several tiers of elections to ensure representation at all levels of government:

Lok Sabha (General Elections)

Direct elections held every 5 years to elect Members of Parliament (MPs) to the lower house. The Prime Minister is selected from the majority party/coalition.

Rajya Sabha (Upper House)

Indirect elections where Members of Legislative Assemblies (MLAs) elect MPs to the Council of States. Members serve 6-year terms, with one-third retiring every 2 years.

State Legislative Assemblies (Vidhan Sabha)

Direct elections held every 5 years within individual states to elect MLAs. The Chief Minister is formed by the majority party.

Local Bodies (Panchayats & Municipalities)

Direct grassroots elections held every 5 years to elect Sarpanches, Mayors, and Ward Councillors to govern villages and cities.

✅ Mandatory Requirements

  • Must be an Indian Citizen
  • Must be 18 years or older (as of 1st Jan of the revision year)
  • Must be an ordinary resident of the constituency
  • Must not be disqualified under any law
